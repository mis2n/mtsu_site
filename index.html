Hello! I am an AI and Machine Learning researcher with a background in physics and Astronomy. My research focuses on interdisciplinary applications of AI and Machine Learning that bridge the gap between state-of-the-art technology and ancient literary texts, specifically for the field of papyrology. A primary focus of my research is the development of image datasets for machine learning through crowdsourcing, and the application of deep learning models to these datasets. Other projects include blockchain and smart contracts, as well as transfer learning and generative AI. I am also interested in the application of AI and Machine Learning to other fields such as physics, chemistry, and astronomy. Other interests include quantum computing & HPC.

I am currently a graduate assistant for Library Technology at the Walker Library of Middle Tennessee State University. In this position I work on a variety of computational projects that involve robotics, microcontrollers (Arduino), microcomputers (Raspberry Pi, Jetson Nano), electrical circuits, and 3D printing for educational and institutional purposes. Some of these projects are featured on my Maker Projects page.
